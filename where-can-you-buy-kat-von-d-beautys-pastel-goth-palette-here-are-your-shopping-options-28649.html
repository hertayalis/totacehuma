Where Can You Buy Kat Von D Beauty's Pastel Goth Palette? Here Are Your Shopping Options

 Posted on May 30, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  417 words  |  Aldo Pusey

In case you missed it, Kat Von D Beauty is at it again. The brand created the Pastel Goth Palette that's colorful and completely wearable at the same time. With all the sneak peeks they've shown, you might think you know everything about the shadow kit. Well, think again. According to Instagram, the Pastel Goth Palette launched early. Where can you buy Kat Von D Beauty's latest launch, you ask? Here are all your shopping options, depending how eager you are to buy.

If you've been patiently awaiting the arrival of Kat Von D Beauty's latest eyeshadow palette, then I have some exciting news. The Pastel Goth Palette is available on the Kat Von D Beauty site right now. The brand completely surprised their fans on Jan. 7 with the announcement.

I know what you're thinking. You might have been under the impression that the Pastel Goth Palette was supposed to launch on Jan. 10 at Sephora. Seeing as the brand posted the date along with all of their Instagram photos. Don't worry, because that's still going to happen. But for all of you who can't wait that long, you can shop right away on the Kat Von D Beauty Site.

Pastel Goth Eyeshadow Palette, $38, Kat Von D Beauty

As far as where to buy, that's completely up to you. The palette is $38 on both sites and you'll get free shipping over $50 from both online shops as well. The only different is that Sephora's is free standard shipping, while KVD will get it to you in three days.

There's also Sephora's point perk. If you're signed up for the Beauty Insider, you'll get points to use towards future goodies. The only perk on the Kat Von D Beauty website is that you'll get a free mini liner with purchase of $50 or more, so you'll have to add some more to your basket to get your reward.

Pastel Goth Eyeshadow Palette, $38, Kat Von D Beauty

No matter where you buy, you can't go wrong with this palette. What the brand describes as "edgy neutrals" are absolutely stunning on every skin tone and can be used in so many of different way. If I were you, I'd go snag it before it's too late.
