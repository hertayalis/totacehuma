Jalen Ramsey career

Ramsey was predicted to be selected in the first round of the NFL Draft after graduating from Florida State. Daniel Jeremiah, Charles Davis, and Mike Mayock, three NFL media analysts, ranked him among the top 10 choices.

He was given a pass to the NFL combine, participated in all of the positional drills, finished first in the broad jump, and tied for first in the vertical jump.

Ramsey was taken by the Jacksonville Jaguars with the fifth overall choice in the first round of the 2016 NFL Draft. The draft’s highest-ever cornerback selection, he was. In the history of the Jaguars’ club, he was selected as the first cornerback.

During rookie training camp on May 19, 2016, Ramsey had a minor meniscus tear. During his second year of high school, he underwent microfracture surgery on the same knee.

Ramsey signed a four-year, $23.35 million contract with the Jaguars on May 22, 2016, which included a signing bonus of $15.18 million and a $22.90 million guarantee. Ramsey had surgery to fix the torn meniscus on May 24, 2016.

Ramsey rejoined a reorganized secondary that includes A. J. Bouye, Aaron Colvin, Barry Church, and Tashaun Gipson as the Jaguars’ de facto starting cornerback.

Prior to the season, NFL analyst Ike Taylor ranked them as the seventh-best secondary and Ramsey and Bouye as the finest cornerback duo.

Ramsey has 65 total tackles, three interceptions, and 13 passes defended in the Jaguars’ 5-11 season in 2018, in which he started all 16 games.

On April 25, 2019, the Jaguars exercised Ramsey’s fifth-year option. Ramsey started the first three games for the Jaguars and finished with 13 solo tackles, a pass deflection, a forced fumble, and a sack.

On October 15, 2019, Ramsey was exchanged for a 2020 first-round selection (later used on K’Lavon Chaisson), a 2021 first-round pick (later used on Travis Etienne), and a 2021 fourth-round pick and sent to the Los Angeles Rams.

Ramsey and the Rams agreed to a five-year, $105 million agreement on September 9, 2020, with $71.2 million of that amount being guaranteed. He became the highest-paid defensive back in NFL history thanks to the contract.

In accordance with the new NFL jersey number rule, Ramsey declared during the Rams minicamp on June 13, 2021, that he will officially change his number to 5.

Ramsey recorded 77 tackles and four interceptions towards the end of the regular season in 2021. Ramsey won his first Super Bowl title after helping the Rams upset the Cincinnati Bengals 23-20 in the championship game.

Ramsey recorded a total of four tackles and one pass breakup in the Super Bowl. He was named to the First Team All-Pro and was nominated for the 2021 Pro Bowl. On the list of the NFL’s top 100 players for 2022, he was voted ninth by his fellow players.

Ramsey had shoulder surgery on June 21. Ramsey suffered tears on both shoulders for the whole 2021 season, it was revealed.

Ramsey was dealt to the Miami Dolphins on March 15, 2023, along with tight end Hunter Long and a third-round draft pick that had originally come from the New England Patriots as part of the DeVante Parker deal.
